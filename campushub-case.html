CampusHub case study.

CampusHub is a student-focused web platform designed to bring campus events, notices, and opportunities into one clean place. The project was built to reduce information chaos and make it easier for students to discover what matters without jumping across scattered groups or channels.

Problem

Students usually get updates through posters, WhatsApp groups, separate notices, and word of mouth. That makes event discovery fragmented and causes important information to be missed.

Approach

I focused on a single interface where events, opportunities, and updates could live together with cleaner structure, better discoverability, and a more usable student experience.

Outcome

The project became a strong portfolio piece because it combines practical product thinking with full stack implementation, interface design, and real-world use-case clarity.

Inside The Build
UI + Notes

Design Direction

The interface was planned to feel direct and practical, with less clutter and faster scanning so students can immediately find what is happening on campus.

Tech Decisions

The stack was chosen to support a responsive frontend, manageable backend logic, and a simple path to extend features like authentication, listings, and admin workflows.
